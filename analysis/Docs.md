For my analysis I wanted to answer the following questions: What are the markers of an at-risk person to fall into opioid-use disorder? Does the distribution of 
naloxone have an impact on opioid deaths? What type of opioids are people dying from the most and how are they obtained? What are common circumstances surrounding 
overdose deaths? Where is the most illegal possession of opioids in Montgomery County? 
Key findings:
Parafluorofentanyl is very common among opioid deaths; however, there is no correlation between any of the opioids and overall deaths. This shows that if overall 
deaths increase other opioids do not significantly increase or decrease. I found it iknteresting that parafluorofentanyl has the slightest negative relationship, 
meaning that as opioid death increases, parafluorofentanyl death ever so slighty decreases. I would not expect this given it's prevalence across the US.
There is a strong relationship bewteen people who have misused opioids in the past year, and people who recieve government assistance. There is a strong relationship 
with people who would not work for a company that screens for drugs before hiring. I am not suprised people arrested for drug posession/sale, and people arrested for
DUI is significantly related to people who misuse opioids.
There are 6 significant circymstances that occur: those who have had an overdose in the past month, a prior overdose at all, currently in substance use disorder
treatment, evidence of illegal drugs involved, recently released from an institutional setting, ever been treated for mental health or substance use disorder, and 
had at least one opportunity for intervention. All of the relationships are positive meaning they are all assosciated with an increase in deaths. This indicates that
treatments were unsuccessful, and there is inadequate procedures following an overdose, since a significant amount of people who died from an overdoses before, and
even more in the past month. A lot of people seem to have been in some type of treatment suggesting that the issue isn't getting people there, it's succeeding in 
treating the patients.
There is no significant relationship between naloxone distribution and opioid deaths, indicating existing efforts to decreease unintentional overdoses are 
ineffective.
Silver Spring appears to have the highest occorences of opioid crimes, next to Rockville, Germantown, and Gaithersburg.
