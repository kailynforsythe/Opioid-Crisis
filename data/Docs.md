I will be using 4 sources
National Survey on Drug Use and Health from the Sustance Abuseand Mental Health Services Administration:
https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health/datafiles

NSDUH surveys a random population sample of approximately 70,000 people every year. The survey consists of 2,638 columns, te majority are direct questions the 
participants were asked. There are columns of data that the SAMHSA manipulated by combining columns in a meaningful way so that it is easier for users to derive 
conclusions. I will only be using 38 columns that I have selected, each are directly related to opioids or, defining characteristerics often assosciated with drug 
abusers.

State Unintentional Drug Overdose Reporting System from the Center for Disease Control and prevention: 
https://www.cdc.gov/overdose-prevention/data-research/facts-stats/sudors-dashboard-fatal-overdose-data.html

SUDORS takes their data from death certificates, medical examiner or coroner reports, and postmortem toxicology results. It only includes unintential or undetermend 
intent deaths of all drugs. It does include data about opioids in detail in addition to other substances. SUDORS has data from every state from 2020-2024 with over
270 columns. It is worth taking into account that this data only includes unintentional deaths so we are not seeing what opioids are being used in suicides. 

Naloxone Dispensing Ratea from the Center for Disease Control and prevention: 
https://www.cdc.gov/overdose-prevention/data-research/facts-stats/naloxone-dispensing-rate-maps.html

This data includes rate of Naloxone distribution per 100 people for each state from 2019-2024. Naloxone, also known as narcan, is a life saving drug administered at
the beginning of an overdose to reverse respitory depression. Narcan is often handed out for free by agencies like the fire department or can be bought.

Opioid Crimes from DataMontgomery: 
https://data.montgomerycountymd.gov/Public-Safety/Opioid-Crime-Incidents-Jan-1-2017-Present/4g4c-7xsi/about_data

Opioid  crimes is collected by the Montgomery County Police Department. Every charge is a crime against society, then it is either a drug/narcotic violations or drug
equipment violations, the thrid crime name goes into specifics about type and wither they're charged with possesion or sale. This data spans from 2017 to present 
with 17 columns and 23,859 rows.
